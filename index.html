How many ping pong balls would it take to fill an average-sized school bus? Describe each step in your thought process.

1. Measurements

First we need to know how big a ping pong ball and a school bus are.

2. Arrangement

Next, we need to determine how the ping pong balls will be arranged to fit to maximize volume. What happens to work out well is a cube of three layers consisting of 5 balls, 4 balls, and 5 balls respectively. Each side looks like this.

3. Trigonometry

With a little bit of math, we can determine the length of a side of this cube.

4. Divy up the Space

Now we imagine a school bus as a rectangular prism, and for convenience two of its dimensions are roughly 1 meter.

5. Calculate our Cubes

If we take our two same-sized dimensions of the prism to be made up of 12 cubes each, we can divide the volume by those two lengths, and determine how long the other side should be, which in our case is 68.6 meters. Dividing this by the length of a cube, we can now find the number of cubes that can fit along our third side.

6. Tally up the Numbers

Now that we know how many of our packed cubes can fit into our prism, we can do some quick calculations to figure out the number of balls!

7. Precision/Accuracy

If my calculations are precise, there is a bit of room leftover at the end: just enough to fit one 12 by 12 row of 4-ball groups. This would add 576(144*4) balls to our total bringing it to 1,629,504!

Create a liquid layout with HTML, CSS, and Javascript. This layout must support the following resolutions: 1024x768, 1680x1050. Explain why you would use a liquid layout.

There are so many different device/browser combinations it's mind boggling. Practically every single person's setup for veiwing web content is unique. When one is designing a website, it is important that it work with all possible (or at the very least, likely) combinations. A liquid layout based on scaling objects via screen percentages is a nice way to make sure your content looks consistent across platforms!
